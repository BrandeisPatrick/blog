From zero to forward deployed

The forward deployed engineer job as a small app: six screens, tap through them — what the job is, the skills it takes, the road from zero, and where you are on it.

The job

The gap is the job

Every AI product exists twice: in the demo, and inside the customer's stack. An MIT-affiliated study found ~95% of enterprise AI pilots never reach measurable impact. The forward deployed engineer is the vendor's answer: embedded with the customer, part engineer, part consultant, part account owner.

The skill radar

Six axes repeat in every 2026 FDE job post. Toggle the profiles; tap any axis label for the concrete skills and the interview round that probes them. The gap between the two shapes is the curriculum.

The road from zero

Five stages, each ending in something shipped. Tap a station. The pin is placed by the quiz on the next screen — take it.

Where do you start?

Twelve honest checks, four rounds. Nothing is stored or sent — your browser talking to itself.

The interview funnel

5–8 stages over 3–6 weeks, roughly half of it not coding. Tap a gate. The red one decides more than the rest combined.

Coding rounds are practical — messy CSVs, small pipelines, rate limiters — so LeetCode grinding is misallocated prep. Some companies swap a round for a 24–48h build against a mock customer dataset.

The market, in two panels

Counts from JobsByCulture; comp from 2026 salary guides — AI-lab / Palantir tier; startups pay meaningfully less, and trackers disagree. Directional, not audited.

Three projects that signal

RAG over hostile data

Scanned PDFs, an old wiki export, inconsistent CSVs — retrieval plus a scored eval set with a regression gate.signal: eval engineering — the #1 hand-wave interviewers punish

An agent with a paper trail

A tool-calling workflow doing something real, with traces, cost/latency budgets, guardrails, a failure-mode table.signal: "here's how I know when it breaks" — operator thinking

An integration you didn't control

Ship into a system you don't own — their SSO, their review, their constraints — then write the war story.signal: the actual job, miniaturized

Fine print

The title is having a moment — some "FDE" posts are rebadged solutions engineering; the tells that it's real are embedded customer time, code ownership, and a product feedback loop. The job is travel and tension — the same embedded model that makes it a founder factory makes it a grind. This is a snapshot — mid-2026 numbers will drift; the six radar axes drift much slower.
